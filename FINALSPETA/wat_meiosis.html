What is Meiosis?

Foundation of All Life

Definition:

Meiosis is a type of cell division that reduces the number of chromosomes by half, resulting in the formation of four genetically unique haploid cells (gametes). It occurs in sexually reproducing organisms and is essential for genetic diversity.

Function:

  • Produces gametes (sperm and egg cells)
  • Ensures genetic variation
  • Maintains chromosome number in species across generations
